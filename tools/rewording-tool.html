Rewording is the process of altering the phrasing of a piece of text while retaining its original meaning. It involves changing the structure, vocabulary, and sometimes even the tone of the content, but the core message remains intact. This technique is commonly used to make content more readable, improve clarity, or avoid plagiarism.

Rewording and paraphrasing are similar techniques with distinct purposes:

  1. 1. Rewording: Involves changing specific words or phrases in a sentence while retaining the original meaning. It's typically used to enhance clarity, improve readability, or tailor content for a specific audience.   2. 2. Paraphrasing: Rephrases entire sentences or passages in your own words. The goal is to express the same idea differently, often simplifying complex concepts or adapting content to a new context. Paraphrasing involves more substantial changes to the structure and flow of the text.
